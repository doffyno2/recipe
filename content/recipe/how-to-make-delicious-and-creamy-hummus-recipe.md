It is a healthy and tasty addition to any meal or snack time. In this article, we will provide you with a detailed recipe on how to make hummus from scratch, including the ingredients, marinade, kitchen tools needed, cooking instructions, and frequently asked questions.</p>

<h2>Ingredients Needed to Make Hummus Recipe</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Ingredients%20Needed%20To%20Make%20Hummus%20Recipe&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Ingredients Needed To Make Hummus Recipe" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>Before we dive into the recipe, let's take a look at the ingredients needed to make hummus:</p>

<ul>
    <li>1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed</li>
    <li>1/4 cup tahini paste</li>
    <li>2 garlic cloves, minced</li>
    <li>3 tbsp lemon juice</li>
    <li>1/4 tsp cumin powder</li>
    <li>1/4 tsp paprika powder</li>
    <li>1/4 tsp salt</li>
    <li>2 tbsp olive oil</li>
    <li>2-3 tbsp water</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Make Hummus Recipe</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=How%20To%20Make%20Hummus%20Recipe&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="How To Make Hummus Recipe" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>Now, let's get to the main part of the recipe, how to make hummus:</p>

<ol>
    <li>Add the chickpeas, tahini paste, garlic, lemon juice, cumin powder, paprika powder, salt, and olive oil to a food processor or blender.</li>
    <li>Blend until smooth, scraping the sides occasionally.</li>
    <li>If the hummus is too thick, add 2-3 tablespoons of water to thin it out.</li>
    <li>For the marinade, combine the olive oil, cumin powder, and paprika powder in a small bowl.</li>
    <li>Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl and drizzle the marinade on top.</li>
    <li>Garnish with some fresh parsley and serve with pita bread, crackers, or vegetable sticks.</li>
</ol>

<h2>FAQs</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Faqs%20Hummus%20Recipe&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Faqs Hummus Recipe" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>Here are some frequently asked questions about hummus:</p>

<h3>1. Is hummus healthy?</h3>

<p>Yes, hummus is a healthy and nutritious dip. It is high in protein, fiber, and healthy fats.</p>

<h3>2. Is tahini paste necessary for hummus?</h3>

<p>Yes, tahini paste is an essential ingredient in hummus. It adds a nutty and creamy flavor to the dip.</p>

<h3>3. Can I use canned chickpeas for hummus?</h3>

<p>Yes, canned chickpeas are a convenient option for making hummus. Just make sure to rinse and drain them before using.</p>

<h3>4. How long does hummus last in the refrigerator?</h3>

<p>Hummus can last in the refrigerator for up to a week if stored in an airtight container.</p>

<h3>5. Can I freeze hummus?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can freeze hummus for up to 3 months. Just make sure to thaw it in the refrigerator overnight before using.</p>

<h3>6. Can I customize the flavor of hummus?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can experiment with different spices and herbs to customize the flavor of hummus. Some popular options include roasted red peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, and basil.</p>

<h3>7. Can I make hummus without a food processor?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can make hummus without a food processor by using a blender or even a potato masher. However, the texture may not be as smooth as when using a food processor.</p>

<h3>8. Is hummus vegan?</h3>

<p>Yes, hummus is a vegan-friendly dip as it does not contain any animal products.</p>

<h3>9. Can I use olive oil instead of tahini paste?</h3>

<p>No, olive oil cannot be used as a substitute for tahini paste as they have different flavors and textures.</p>

<h3>10. Can I use other beans instead of chickpeas?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can use other beans like black beans or white beans to make hummus, but it may change the flavor and texture of the dip.</p>
