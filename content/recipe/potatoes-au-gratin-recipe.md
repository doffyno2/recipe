<p>If you're looking for a delicious side dish that will impress your guests, look no further than potatoes au gratin. This classic French dish is made with thinly sliced potatoes, cheese, and a creamy sauce. It's perfect for special occasions or whenever you want to elevate your dinner game. In this article, we'll show you how to make potatoes au gratin from scratch. Let's get started!</p>

<h2>Ingredients Needed to Make Potatoes Au Gratin Recipe</h2>
<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Ingredients%20Image&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Ingredients Image" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>Before we dive into the recipe, let's take a look at the ingredients you'll need:</p>

<ul>
  <li>2 pounds potatoes</li>
  <li>1 1/2 cups heavy cream</li>
  <li>1 1/2 cups whole milk</li>
  <li>4 cloves garlic, minced</li>
  <li>1 teaspoon salt</li>
  <li>1/2 teaspoon black pepper</li>
  <li>2 cups grated Gruyere cheese</li>
  <li>1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese</li>
  <li>2 tablespoons butter, cut into small pieces</li>
</ul>

  <li>A baking dish</li>
  <li>Foil or a lid to cover the baking dish</li>

<h2>How to Make Potatoes Au Gratin Recipe</h2>
<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=How%20To%20Make%20Image&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="How To Make Image" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>Now that we have all of our ingredients and tools ready, let's start making potatoes au gratin:</p>

<ol>
  <li>Preheat your oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).</li>
  <li>Peel the potatoes and slice them thinly using a mandoline or sharp knife.</li>
  <li>In a large saucepan, heat the heavy cream, whole milk, garlic, salt, and black pepper over medium heat. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes.</li>
  <li>Grease a baking dish with butter and arrange a layer of potato slices on the bottom.</li>
  <li>Pour some of the cream mixture over the potatoes, then sprinkle some of the Gruyere and Parmesan cheeses on top.</li>
  <li>Repeat the layering process with the remaining potatoes, cream mixture, and cheese.</li>
  <li>Dot the top of the potatoes with small pieces of butter.</li>
  <li>Cover the baking dish with foil or a lid and bake for 45 minutes.</li>
  <li>Remove the foil or lid and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the potatoes are tender.</li>
  <li>Let the potatoes au gratin cool for a few minutes before serving.</li>
</ol>
