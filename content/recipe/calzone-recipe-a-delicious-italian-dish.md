<h2>Ingredients Needed to Make Calzone Recipe</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Calzone%20Ingredients&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Calzone Ingredients" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>Before you start making your calzone, you need to gather all the necessary ingredients. Here is a list of the ingredients you'll need:</p>

<ul>
  <li>1 pound pizza dough</li>
  <li>1/2 cup marinara sauce</li>
  <li>1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese</li>
  <li>1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese</li>
  <li>3/4 cup cooked and crumbled Italian sausage</li>
  <li>1/2 cup sliced mushrooms</li>
  <li>1/4 cup diced onions</li>
  <li>1 minced garlic clove</li>
  <li>1 beaten egg</li>
  <li>1 tablespoon olive oil</li>
  <li>Salt and pepper</li>
</ul>

<h2>For the Marinade Calzone Recipe</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Calzone%20Marinade&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Calzone Marinade" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>While the calzone doesn't require a marinade, you can add some extra flavor by brushing the dough with a mixture of olive oil, garlic, and Italian seasoning. Here's how to make the marinade:</p>

<ul>
  <li>1/4 cup olive oil</li>
  <li>2 minced garlic cloves</li>
  <li>1 teaspoon Italian seasoning</li>
  <li>1/4 teaspoon salt</li>
  <li>1/4 teaspoon black pepper</li>
</ul>

<p>Whisk all the ingredients together in a small bowl until well combined. Brush the mixture onto the dough before adding the filling.</p>

<h2>How to Make Calzone Recipe</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=How%20To%20Make%20Calzone&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="How To Make Calzone" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>Now that you have all your ingredients and tools, it's time to make the calzone. Follow these steps:</p>

<ol>
  <li>Preheat your oven to 425°F.</li>
  <li>Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface until it's about 1/4 inch thick.</li>
  <li>Place the rolled out dough on a parchment-lined baking sheet or pizza stone.</li>
  <li>Spread the marinara sauce over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.</li>
  <li>Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella and Parmesan cheese over the sauce.</li>
  <li>Add the cooked Italian sausage, mushrooms, onions, and garlic on top of the cheese.</li>
  <li>Brush the edges of the dough with the beaten egg.</li>
  <li>Fold the dough in half and press the edges together to seal.</li>
  <li>Brush the top of the calzone with the egg wash.</li>
  <li>Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the calzone is golden brown and crispy.</li>
  <li>Let the calzone cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.</li>
</ol>

<ol>

  <li>Place the leftover calzone on a baking sheet or pizza stone.</li>
  <li>Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the calzone is heated through.</li>
</ol>

<h3>1. Can I use store-bought pizza dough?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can use store-bought pizza dough instead of making your own. Just make sure to follow the package instructions for baking.</p>

<h3>2. Can I freeze calzone?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can freeze calzone. Wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil before freezing. When you're ready to eat it, let it thaw in the fridge overnight before reheating.</p>

<h3>3. Can I use different fillings?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can customize your calzone with different fillings. Some popular options include pepperoni, ham, bell peppers, spinach, and ricotta cheese.</p>

<h3>4. Can I make calzone ahead of time?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can make calzone ahead of time and store it in the fridge for up to 24 hours before baking. Just make sure to cover it tightly with plastic wrap.</p>

<p>Yes, you can use a different sauce instead of marinara. Pesto, Alfredo, or BBQ sauce are all great options.</p>

<p>The calzone should be golden brown and crispy on the outside. You can also check the internal temperature with a meat thermometer – it should be at least 165°F.</p>
